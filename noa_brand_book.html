01 — Brand Story

Where metal
meets meaning

Noa Unisex Jewelry is the studio practice of Noa Shechter — a Tel Aviv–based metalsmith and goldsmith who found her calling at the workbench. Starting with a single ring, she fell in love with the act of turning raw metal into something wearable, permanent, and quietly personal.

Every piece is conceived and finished by hand in her home studio. Twice a week, Noa brings her work to the Nahalat Binyamin Art Market — one of Tel Aviv's oldest and most beloved creative spaces — where she meets her customers face to face. Her Etsy shop extends that intimacy online.

"My style is a little eclectic — yet very earthy and natural, with smart designs and clean cuts. Less is more."

02 — Logo & Visual Identity

The mark

The logo is a hand-drawn calligraphic signature — the word "noa" rendered in a single gestural sweep, with the dot of the final letter forming a small star that trails off into a fine line. The mark is organic, authored, and irreproducible. It communicates craft, personality, and Tel Aviv's creative spirit in a single stroke.

This mark should always be used as the placed SVG file and never redrawn, retyped, or approximated in any other typeface.

On white
On dark
On cream
Use

Always use the original SVG or supplied PNG. Maintain generous clear space equal to the mark's height on all sides. Use on white, cream, or deep charcoal only.

Never

Never redraw, retype, stretch, recolor, outline, or place on a busy background without a clean, uncluttered field behind the mark.

03 — Color Palette

The palette

The palette is drawn directly from the materials — ink, gold, raw silver, warm paper, aged stone. Deliberately restrained. White is the primary canvas. The golds are the only accent. All supporting tones are neutral, neither warm nor cold — so the work reads as made for everyone.

Usage rule

White is the primary canvas. Surface Gray (#efefef) carries secondary sections and breathing room. Studio Black is the ink for all text, the logo, and primary UI. Heritage Gold appears only as a metal reference — never as a UI accent. Borders always use Rule Gray at 1px. Radius: 0px throughout.

The palette is monochrome minimalist by design — gallery-grade, unisex, gender-balanced. Warmth comes from product photography and handwriting, never from chrome or UI.

04 — Typography

The type

Two typefaces carry the full typographic weight of the brand. Catamaran sets every display, heading, and label — uppercase, tightly tracked, structural. Instrument Sans carries all body copy and UI with a neutral, precise voice. Libre Bodoni remains as an editorial italic for long-form story moments only. The pairing is intentional: geometric sans for architecture, humanist sans for information, serif only where emotion is earned.

05 — Brand Voice & Messaging

How Noa speaks

The brand voice is the voice of an artist who loves her craft and trusts her customers to feel it. Quiet confidence. No performance. No exclamation marks. The work speaks for itself — the words simply open the door.

"Follow your heart. I found what is my real love. Now my soul is much happier."
Earthy & honest

Talk about materials and process plainly. Gold, silver, the flame, the hammer. Real words for real things.

Quietly confident

No superlatives, no exclamations. Let the work speak. Confidence comes from restraint, not volume.

Inclusive warmth

Unisex by design and by spirit. "Made for women and men as one." Never gender the piece or the customer.

Personal & human

Share the process. The studio. The market stall. Authenticity is the differentiator — don't polish it away.

Copy in practice

Write this

"Hammered from solid 14k gold. Fits like it was always there."

Not this

"STUNNING gold ring — perfect for any occasion!! You'll LOVE it!!"

Write this

"Wear it on whichever finger feels right. Made for everyone."

Not this

"The perfect gift for him or the special lady in your life."

06 — Photography Style

The image language

Photography should feel like natural light falling on a studio workbench — warm, unhurried, close. The goal is to show jewelry as it actually lives: against skin, on hands, in the context of the creative space where it was made.

01
Natural light only

Window light or open shade. No harsh flash. Warmth and softness are non-negotiable.

02
Warm neutral grounds

Linen, raw concrete, aged wood, bare skin. Never bright white or saturated color.

03
On-body priority

Show pieces on real hands and necks. Mix product-only and worn shots at 1:2 ratio.

04
Process & studio

Document the making: tools, sketches, the market stall. Authenticity is the product.

05
Shallow focus

Focus precisely on the piece. Let surroundings soften into warmth behind it.

06
Unisex casting

Feature all genders and expressions. The jewelry is for everyone — so is the imagery.

Post-processing

Minimal editing. Lift shadows slightly for warmth. Add warmth to midranges. Never over-saturate or cool the tone. The metal should look like metal — not a rendering.

07 — Product Collections

The collections

Each collection is built around a single technique or concept. The line holds together because every piece shares the same sensibility — not because everything matches. Collections are named directly, without marketing inflation.

Wires
Delicate wire-formed structures — rings, pendants, earrings built from drawn metal line.
Grid
Geometric lattice forms. Structured, architectural, rooted in pattern and repetition.
Fold Forming
Sheet metal folded cold. Each piece carries the memory of its forming process.
Color Blocks
Mixed-material pieces with bold accent elements. Playful yet restrained.
Spirals
Coiled and wound forms. Organic movement frozen in silver and gold.
Signet Rings
Minimal flat-top signets for all genders. The most everyday piece in the line.
One of a Kind
Unique experimental works. Available only once. The purest expression of the studio.
Earrings
Across all techniques. Lightweight, graphic, wearable alone or stacked.
Necklaces & Bracelets
Understated chains and cuffs that complement rather than compete.

Naming convention

Name pieces by material + form + collection. Example: Gold Wire Twist Ring — Wires Collection. Avoid evocative names that gender or over-romanticize the work.
